Long before there were exams, there was stardust. The same atoms that once burned inside stars now sit, and think, and wonder.

To study is not to compete with anyone. It is to pay attention — and attention is how the cosmos comes to experience itself, one curious mind at a time. You are not preparing against the universe. You are the universe, preparing.

The four stars of Tier I

One small constellation to find your way by.

Four subjects, reframed as four ways of looking up. Each is a doorway into the same wonder.

Quantitative Aptitude

The grammar of orbits

Number is the oldest language we know. The same ratios that hold planets in their paths hold the logic of every sum. Learn to read them, and arithmetic stops being a wall and becomes a window.

General Intelligence & Reasoning

Patterns in the dark

Early people looked up at scattered light and found order — animals, heroes, maps. Reasoning is that same instinct: drawing the line that was always there, waiting to be seen.

General Awareness

The long memory

From cooling stars to rivers, from rivers to cities and laws — the whole human story carried forward. To know it is to hold a little of everything that brought you here.

English Comprehension

The naming of things

Language is how the universe describes itself back to itself. Read closely, and meaning opens like a sky clearing after rain.
